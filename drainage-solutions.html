Sound Familiar?

Signs You Have a Drainage Problem

These are the warning signs Tampa Bay homeowners can't afford to ignore. Every rainstorm makes them worse.

💧

Standing Water After Rain

Puddles that don't drain within 24 hours are a clear sign water has nowhere to go — and a breeding ground for mosquitoes.

🏠

Water Near Your Foundation

Pooling near your home's base puts pressure on concrete, causes cracks, and can lead to costly structural repairs.

🧞

Muddy, Unusable Yard

If your backyard turns into a swamp after every storm, you're losing valuable outdoor living space every rainy season.

🌿

Dead or Patchy Grass

Waterlogged soil suffocates grass roots. If your lawn looks like a patchwork quilt, poor drainage is likely the culprit.

⛰

Soil Erosion

Topsoil washing away after every rain event strips nutrients, exposes roots, and gradually destabilizes your property.

🪲

Mosquito Breeding Grounds

In Florida, standing water is a mosquito paradise. Eliminate it at the source with a professionally designed drainage system.

If any of these sound familiar, we can help — starting with a free on-site analysis.

Common Questions

Drainage FAQs

French drains handle subsurface water — they use a sloped gravel trench and perforated pipe to redirect water traveling underground. Catch basins handle surface water — they're collection points that grab runoff from the surface and channel it away. Many of our projects use both together for complete protection against Tampa Bay's heavy rainfall.

Costs vary based on the size of your property and the type of system required. A simple catch basin starts around $800, French drains typically run $1,500–$3,000, and complete multi-system installs can range from $3,000–$8,000+. We provide free, no-obligation on-site estimates so you'll know exactly what to expect before any work begins.

Most residential drainage projects are completed in 1–3 days depending on scope. A single catch basin or channel drain can often be done in one day. Larger French drain systems or full yard regrading may take 2–4 days. We'll give you a clear timeline before we start and work efficiently to minimize disruption to your daily routine.

Professionally installed drainage systems can last 20–30 years or more with proper care. We use commercial-grade materials rated for Florida's demanding climate. We recommend a quick annual check to clear debris from drain openings and confirm flow isn't obstructed — which takes about 15 minutes.

Yes — for larger drainage projects, permits are typically required by Hillsborough, Pinellas, and Pasco counties. We handle the entire permit process for you, including submission, inspections, and sign-off. You won't need to deal with the county office at all. Permit costs are included in your estimate upfront.

We take great care to minimize landscape disruption. Our crews work around existing plants, trees, and hardscape features whenever possible. After installation, we restore the disturbed areas — replacing sod, raking, and cleaning up completely. Most customers are surprised at how clean the site looks when we're done.
