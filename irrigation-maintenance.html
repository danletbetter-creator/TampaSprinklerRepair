Seasonal Maintenance

Spring Preparation (March-May)

Spring is critical for Tampa irrigation. After winter dormancy, systems need inspection and adjustment for the growing season ahead.

🔧 What We Do

  • Complete system inspection for winter damage
  • Pressure testing all zones
  • Clean/replace filters
  • Check and adjust spray patterns
  • Valve lubrication and operation check
  • Winterization equipment removal
  • Program controller for spring watering
  • Leak detection sweep

📋 Why Spring Matters

Winter freezes can crack PVC lines, freeze valves, and damage sprinkler heads. Spring inspection catches these problems before they waste water all season. We also adjust your system for spring growth, when Saint Augustine grass needs more water.

Cost if skipped: $500-2,000 in wasted water and missed repairs.

Spring inspection package: $299 (included in all maintenance plans).

Peak Season Care

Summer Optimization (June-August)

Tampa's peak heat and afternoon thunderstorms require active monitoring and adjustment. We prevent over-watering and storm damage.

☀️ Summer Challenges

  • Heat stress on Saint Augustine grass
  • Daily thunderstorms causing over-watering
  • High evaporation rates
  • Increased fungal disease risk
  • Pressure surges during storms
  • Algae growth in wet zones

🌊 Our Solutions

We adjust watering schedules to account for daily rain, install/optimize rain sensors, and monitor weather patterns. This prevents the over-watering that leads to fungal disease and wasted water.

Summer maintenance includes bi-weekly adjustments, pressure testing after storms, and drainage checks to prevent flooding.

Summer service package: $50/year add-on (or included in Premium/Elite plans).

Transition Period

Fall Adjustments (September-November)

Fall is when water restrictions increase and temperatures cool. We transition your system from summer survival mode to fall efficiency.

🍂 Fall Maintenance

  • Water restriction compliance adjustment
  • Program adjustment for cooler weather
  • Post-drought system check
  • Aeration & thatch management
  • Fertilization schedule review
  • Winterization preparation begins
  • Controller programming update

🔄 Fall Transition Details

As temperatures drop and water restrictions may tighten, we adjust your system for efficiency. Fall is also when grass enters dormancy preparation, requiring less water but better quality coverage.

We typically reduce watering frequency by 20-30% in fall while maintaining lawn health.

Fall maintenance package: $299 (included in all plans).

Cold Protection

Winter Protection (December-February)

Even rare Tampa freezes can crack irrigation lines. Winter maintenance protects your system during dormancy and prevents freeze damage.

❄️ Winter Services

  • Pre-freeze system inspection
  • Backflow preventer protection
  • Line draining (if needed)
  • Valve winterization
  • Controller adjustments for dormancy
  • Minimal watering schedule
  • Post-freeze damage assessment

❓ Tampa's Freeze Risk

While Tampa rarely experiences freezing temperatures, when it happens (typically January-February), unprotected irrigation systems crack and fail. Water expands when frozen, creating cracks in PVC lines that leak all year.

Winter maintenance typically means minimal watering (dormant grass needs little water) and protection measures for exposed components.

Winter inspection & protection: Included in all plans.

Irrigation Maintenance FAQ

How often should irrigation systems be serviced?

Professionally designed systems should receive at least quarterly (4x/year) inspection and seasonal adjustments. Our Essential plan includes this. Premium plans provide bi-monthly or monthly service for more complex systems.

What's included in a typical maintenance visit?

We inspect all components (valves, heads, lines, controllers), test water pressure, check for leaks, clean filters, verify coverage patterns, and make seasonal adjustments. Visit length: 45-90 minutes depending on system complexity.

Can maintenance prevent major repairs?

Yes. About 80% of irrigation failures are preventable through maintenance. Early detection of small problems (loose connections, worn seals, clogged filters) prevents expensive failures later. Maintenance ROI is typically 3-5x the maintenance cost in prevented repairs.

What makes your maintenance different?

We don't just inspect—we optimize. Each visit includes water efficiency analysis, coverage pattern verification, and customized adjustments for Tampa's specific climate. Your system gets better with each maintenance visit, not just checked off.

Do maintenance plans cover emergency repairs?

Emergency repairs (beyond routine maintenance) are not included in plans, but all plan members receive 24/7 priority emergency service and discounts on repairs (20-35% depending on plan).

How do I schedule maintenance?

Simple: book your plan online or call (813) 499-8485. We'll schedule your routine visits, send reminders, and provide online access to service records and recommendations.
