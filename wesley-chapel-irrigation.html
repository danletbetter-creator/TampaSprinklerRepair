Answers to the questions we hear most from Wesley Chapel homeowners.

Do I need a permit to install a new irrigation system in Wesley Chapel?

+

Yes. New irrigation system installations in Pasco County — which includes Wesley Chapel — require permits. Water Oak Irrigation handles all permit acquisition, inspections, and compliance. This is included in our installation estimate at no extra charge. Beware of any contractor who says permits aren't needed — this creates liability for the homeowner.

What are the current watering restrictions in Wesley Chapel?

+

As of 2026, Wesley Chapel falls under Southwest Florida Water Management District (SWFWMD) Modified Phase II restrictions — one designated watering day per week based on your address. The best workaround is using Program A and Program B on your controller to water different areas on different days within the rules. We'll set this up correctly during any service call.

My builder-installed irrigation system isn't covering my lawn evenly — can you fix it?

+

Yes, and this is one of the most common calls we get from Wesley Chapel homeowners. Builder systems are often rushed, with improperly placed heads, mismatched spray patterns, and poorly zoned coverage. We do a full system audit, map every head, and recommend targeted upgrades to give you even, efficient coverage without rewiring everything.

My sprinklers have low pressure in one zone — what's causing it?

+

Low pressure in a single zone is most often caused by an underground line leak, a clogged filter screen on the zone valve, or a partially failed valve. In newer Wesley Chapel homes it can also be from builder-grade fittings that have degraded. We diagnose the root cause before recommending a repair — no guesswork.
